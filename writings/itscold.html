It's Cold


I trudge through the door with some dread in my heart. It's cold.

Knowing the sounds I won't hear anymore. It's cold.

Your presence, your warmth, irritations are gone.

This place that I live in don't seem like a home.

Tender moments, mundane, that mean more 'cause their common.

The places seem empty, those places that saw them.

I'm pretty ok, but malaise draws me in, 'cause it's cold.


Distractions I try, they don't work all that long. It's cold.

My interests I ply, I may listen to songs, but it's cold.

Thoughts are elsewhere, relief! But I've come to expect

interruptions and so then I look to my left

expecting annoyance--instead, I'm bereft. It's cold.


I sit in the love seat that used to hold two. It's cold.

I place my hand down on the warm spot you found, but it's cold.

I shed a few tears, wipe my eyes, blow my nose.

I'll live, I'll move on, I know that's how it goes.

Yet your absence surprises each time that I notice, it's cold.


Where my heart warmed for you, brought a touch or a smile, it is cold.

Even where I wished for a free moment or two, now it's cold.

Yet your memories are here, and the warmth that you brought,

for so long and so clear, it was not all for naught.

It lingers, just not at the front of my thoughts, so it's cold.


This hurt, it will heal, and most sadness will pass, this I know.

That time is not here, I will mourn for you now. It is cold.

You deserve nothing less than this hurt that I feel.

What you've given to me is what this pain reveals.

Love that's new, your warm memories, someday'll overpower

the grief in which now this sad child sit and cowers

no tantrum will speed up these days and these hours.

'Til then, know I love you, I'll treasure you still through the cold.


— For Benga and Cleo
