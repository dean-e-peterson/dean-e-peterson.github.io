Resources

Here are links to resources I have found to be useful and, in some cases, exceptional:

  • Linux Weekly News (LWN) (subscription, but week old content is free) – Very detailed coverage of the Linux kernel and other topics in free and open source software.
  • Mozilla Developer Network (MDN) – Detailed reference on web technologies. Great when you want something more detailed than the also excellent w3schools.
  • SVG Tutorial – Jakob Jenkov's excellent and comprehensive SVG tutorial.
  • Scrum Training Series – An excellent introduction to the SCRUM agile software development methodology.

Dean's Paper Airplane

I designed my own paper airplane. It is not the best paper airplane, but it does fly. The airplane is made with a square piece of paper. Its axis of symmetry is along the diagonal, which is somewhat unusual.

  1. Fold a square sheet of paper in half both horizontally and vertically. (These folds just guide folding the airplane in thirds along the diagonal in a later step.) Unfold and fold the sheet in half along one diagonal. This will be the main axis of symmetry.

  2. Fold the top-right corner down to vertical crease. The fold should reach all the way to the top-left corner.

  3. Fold the bottom-left corner up like the previous step. This should form a flattened cone spreading out from the upper-left corner.

  4. Turn the paper over. Fold the upper-left point down to the middle of the sheet of paper, where the horizontal and vertical creases cross.

  5. Turn the paper over to the original side.

  6. Fold the flap on the top layer of the cone down. The fold should extend from the highest point on the upper-left, to the end of the previous fold on the lower-right.

  7. Temporarily unfold the previous fold. Temporarily unfold the point of the cone back out from where it was folded underneath.

  8. Unfold the entire flap of the cone down.

  9. Fold the other flap of the cone up as shown, from where the crease of the point that was previously folded under meets the edge of the paper on the left, over to where the flap meets the bottom layer of the cone on the right.

  10. No new folds. This step just shows what the sheet of paper would look like now if you unfolded all existing folds.

  11. Unfold the last fold. On the top flap, which is also the top layer, fold the bottom edge of the flap up to the crease from the previous fold.

  12. Repeat the same fold for the other side, on the cone flap that is below the diagonal.

  13. Refold the cone flaps above and below the diagonal as shown.

  14. Here is the tricky step. Fold the point at the upper-left into the cone. When complete it should appear as shown.

  15. Refold the paper along the original diagonal.

  16. Fold the plane in half. Turn it so the original diagonal is at the bottom.

  17. Fold the wing on the top layer down from the top corner at left, such that on the lower right the prior crease reaches just to the bottom of the plane. This wing fold will be close to, but not exactly, parallel to the bottom of the plane.

  18. Turn the plane over. Fold down the wing on the other side. Lift both wings up so that they stick out from the keel. The airplane is ready to fly.
