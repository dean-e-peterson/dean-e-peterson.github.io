Dean's Opinions on Palestine and Israel

Note: I put Palestine first in the title not to give the Palestinian people preference over the Jewish people or the nation state of Israel. I understand the word “Palestine” to mean either the entire area of land or the people group. As such I think “Palestine” is the more general term. For example, 500 years ago, there was no nation state of Israel. The land of Palestine was still there, whatever the identities of the inhabitants.

April 10, 2024 Draft of Proposed Comments to My Senators and Congressperson

Like many Americans, I have watched as Israel suffered a brutal attack by Hamas on October 7 killing over 1,000 Israelis, and as Israel has mounted military operations in Gaza that have killed over 30,000 Palestinians and endangered millions more with famine. I sit enjoying my chicken Caesar salad as Hamas keeps Israeli hostages, thankful that I and my cat have plenty to eat as children in Gaza starve.

So why have I not written you about this before now? I have had a hard time knowing what to demand in the short term. A permanent ceasefire would save so many lives, but if Hamas survived intact, this could all happen again. I also realize that the US influence over Israel is not unlimited, and that we do not want to give Benjamin Netanyahu cover by letting him distract from his corruption and failures by claiming he is standing up for Israelis by standing up to US pressure.

Nevertheless, we need to do what we can. Here, then, are my requests:

  1. Prioritize GREATLY increasing aid to the Palestinians in Gaza. I do not care how, but they need much more aid, not just a little more. I am not naive. I know Hamas may siphon off some of the aid. In the horrible calculus of war, that is regrettable but may be unavoidable. I will be more blunt. If it is a choice, I would rather a few more Hamas fighters survive and end up killing a few more Israeli troops, as horrible as that would be, than to let tens of thousands of Gazan children die, which is a very real possiblity. Even if Gazans voted for Hamas 20 years ago, they do not have a functioning democracy now, and the Gazan children were not even alive then. More aid.

  2. If and when Israel goes into Rafah, demand that Israel enact plans to protect the majority of the civilian population, and to do a better job at protecting civilians than they have to this point in their military campaign.

  3. Longer term, do not give Israel a pass on infringing the rights of Palestinians. Yes, Israel must defend itself, but that does not give Israel carte blanche to dispossess and disenfranchise Palestinian civilians. In particular, Israel should:

    1. STOP building settlements in the West Bank (and do not start building them in Gaza.)

    2. Take concrete steps toward giving Palestinians the democratic right to self-determination. This could be as full citizens in the state of Israel or in a separate Palestinian state as part of a two state solution. However, a single state solution must give Palenstinians the full franchise and vote, and a two state solution must really move forward, and not be forever delayed as the occupation continues.

In short, indefinite occupation and land grabs are not acceptable behavior. At some point, the US must cut off military or other aid to Israel. The US should not participate, even by proxy, in the indefinite occupation of the Palestinian people. Note that even if the US cuts off most aid, we could still sell Israel ammunition for their iron dome to help them defend their civilian population.

Anticipated Objections from Some Supporters of Israel

Why do I mention the Palestinian people and not the Israeli hostages? Because I am confident that Israel is already doing everything they can (as they should) to get those hostages back, moving heaven and earth if necessary. I doubt they need our help with that, but if they do, we should help them. However, it is not OK to prioritize Israeli hostages over far greater numbers of Gazan civilians.

Why do I take such a strong stance against Israeli settlements? Sure, settlements sound innocuous out of context, and I'm sure many wonderful things happen in Israeli settlements like families raising children. However, the overall program is still an unjustified land grab. For comparison, imagine if someone stole your house, then defended their actions by saying they are taking good care of it and are raising a lovely family there. That does not give someone the right to steal your house, and it does not give Israel the right to build settlements on Palestinian land.

I know some believe that all land within Israel's ancient borders belongs to Israel by historical or religious right. Historically, that could be argued of anyone who ever lived anywhere and still survives as a people group. However, even religiously, such an argument is not defensible. Even if you assume Biblical prophesy should have a place in US foreign policy, and even if you assume Biblical prophesy cares so much about specific borders, Biblical prophesies do not come with due dates, and the Bible says that God's time is not our time. Therefore, even from a Biblical perspective, saying that the Israelis need to take over all of Palestine in this century or even in this millenium is adding a non-Biblical human due date to a Biblical prophecy, unilaterally assigning our generation to the task, and then treating that combination as God's Word even in DIRECT CONTRAVENTION of what the Bible unambiguously demands, that we love our neighbor. Perhaps there are things I am not aware of in the Jewish religion that argue for more immediate action, I do not know. However, I still believe in the primacy of being good to your neighbor, and any devoutness that thinks God values borders more than people is not a devotion I feel particularly bound to respect.

To anticipate an argument against me, who am I to tell people what they can and cannot believe religiously? No one special. That is a valid point, although I find it interesting that anyone making it is avoiding the substance of my Biblical argument. Ultimately, people can of course believe what they want to. However, stop demanding that the US government (and, by extension, me) support and participate in your religious land grab at the expense of the livelyhood and human rights of others.

What about the long history of oppression against the Jewish people? What about Israel's survival and security? To that I say: Israel already has enough land inside its pre-1967 borders to be a thriving nation state. They do not need the West Bank and the Gaza strip to survive and thrive. The only thing they still need is security from terrorism, something which should only be enhanced over the long term by improving relations with the Palestinians. After all, Israel has been trying variants of their current strategy for decades, and it has not guaranteed security.

Furthermore, Israel's needs and rights should not come at the expense of the needs and rights of the millions of Palestinians living in the West Bank and Gaza. Palestinians should have the same freedom and self-determination as we want for ourselves and for the Israelis.

Anticipated Objections from Supporters of the Palestinians

Given all of the above, why do I not demand more and sooner? Why do I not demand an immediate, permanent cease fire? Why do I not demand that the US immediately cut off all aid to Israel? Why do I not demand that Israel immediately end its occupation?

First, I still support Israel's existence, and I think there are legitimate security concerns over their hostages and over the continued existence of organized Hamas units. Furthermore, I think the continued existence of Hamas also hurts the Palestinians.

Second, to give people who know much more than I do the time and flexibility to work on things. Perhaps I am naive, and perhaps I do have a Eurocentric, Israel-supporting bias based on where I come from culturally. However, I think about the following: Would pressuring Israel with aid cutoff in 6 months bring about greater changes in Israeli policy than pressuring Israel right now? I don't know, but it is possible, especially since right now Netanyahu might just dig in his heels. If Israel were to immediately withdraw from Gaza and the West Bank, would Hamas or other violent Iranian-supported groups take over? Maybe? I think it would be disasterous if it happened. Is the Palestinian Authority in its current incarnation capable of or supported enough by the Palestinian people to run a new palestinian state if the occupation were ended today? I don't know, and I'd rather have experts and actual stakeholders work on these things.

There are so many things I do not know, but there is one thing I am pretty sure of. The Palestinian people will get one chance at this. For example, consider a two state solution. If the West Bank and the Gaza strip are freed, but are in such a disordered state that violent extremists take over and attack Israel? It's over. The Palestinian people, even ones not remotely involved in such an attack, would have no hope for a free homeland for a generation, if ever. I do not know the best way to move to free Jews and free Palestinians stably coexisting, but I know it won't be easy, and it won't necessarily be quick. However, it needs to happen, and it cannot be infinitely delayed. Therefore, I have tried to demand things that give negotiators room to work, and to make my long term demands about directions, goals, and what features, like Palesinian self determination, that that I would accept in a solution.

Final Clarifications

A couple of final clarifications that I hope are not necessary, but I will make them explicit. I support the existence of Israel. I do not support Hamas.
