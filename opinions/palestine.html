Dean's Opinions on Palestine and Israel

Note: I put Palestine first not to give the Palestinian people preference over the Jewish people or the nation state of Israel, but because by my understanding the word “Palestine” can refer to the entire area of land as well as to the people group, and as such is the more general term. For example, 500 years ago, there was no nation state of Israel. The land of Palestine was still there, whatever the identities of the inhabitants.

March 29, 2024 Draft of Proposed Comments to My Senators and Congressperson

Like many Americans, I have watched as Israel suffered a brutal attack by Hamas on October 7 killing over 1,000 Israelis, and as Israel has mounted military operations in Gaza that have killed over 30,000 Palestinians and endangered millions more with famine. I sit enjoying my chicken Caesar salad as Hamas keeps Israeli hostages, thankful that I and my cat have plenty to eat as children in Gaza starve.

So why have I not written you about this before now? I have had a hard time knowing what to demand in the short term. A permanent ceasefire would save so many lives, but if Hamas broke it, again, this could all happen again. I also realize that we only have so much influence over Israel, and that we do not want to give Benjamin Netanyahu cover by letting him distract from his corruption and failures by claiming he is standing up for Israelis by standing up to foreign pressure.

Nevertheless, we need to do what we can. Here, then, are my requests:

  1. Prioritize GREATLY increasing aid to the Palestinians in Gaza. I do not care how, but they need much more aid, not just a little more. I am not naive. I know Hamas may siphon some of the aid. I will be more blunt. If it is a choice, I would rather a few more Hamas fighters survive and end up killing a few more Israeli troops, as horrible as that would be, than to let tens of thousands of Gazan children die, which is a very real possiblity. Even if Gazans voted for Hamas 20 years ago, they do not have a functioning democracy now, and the Gazan children were not even alive then. More aid.
  2. Longer term, do not give Israel a pass on infringing the rights of Palestinians. Yes, Israel must defend itself, but that does not give Israel carte blanche to dispossess and disenfranchise Palestinian civilians. In particular, Israel should STOP building settlements in the West Bank (and not start building them in Gaza.) Palestinians should have the democratic right of self-determination, whether that be as a separate nation in a two state solution or as full citizens in the state of Israel. Indefinate occupation and land grabs are not acceptable behavior. If we need to cut off military or other aid to Israel, so be it. Even if we need to cut off most aid, I would still support selling Israel ammunition for their iron dome to defend their civilians.

Why do I mention Palestinian people and not Israeli hostages? It is not because I think the Israeli hostages are less important. It is because I am confident that Israel is already doing everything they can (as they should), moving heaven and earth to get those hostages back. I doubt they need our help with that, but if they do, we should help them, but not at the expense of far greater numbers of Gazan civilians.

Why do I take such a strong stance against Israeli settlements? Sure, settlements sound innocuous out of context, and I'm sure many wonderful things happen in Israeli settlements like families raising children. However, the overall program is still an unjustified land grab. For comparison, imagine if someone stole your house, then defended their actions by saying they are taking good care of it and are raising a lovely family there. That does not give someone the right to steal your house, and it does not give Israel the right to build settlements on Palestinian land.

I know some believe Israel has an historical or religious right to all land in their ancient borders. Historically, that could be argued of anyone who ever lived anywhere. Even religiously, such an argument is not defensible. Even if you assume Biblical prophesy cares so much about specific borders, Biblical prophesies do not come with due dates, and the Bible says that God's time is not our time. Saying that the Israelis need to take over all of Palestine in this century or even in this millenium is adding a non-Biblical due date to a Biblical prophecy, unilaterally assigning our generation to the task, then treating it as God's Word even in DIRECT CONTRAVENTION of what the Bible unambiguously demands, that we love our neighbor. Israel has enough land inside its pre-1967 borders to be a thriving nation state without taking land the Palestinians still need to live on. A devoutness that thinks God values borders more than people is not a devotion I feel bound to respect. To anticipate a specious argument against me, who am I to tell people what they can and cannot believe religiously? I am not. People can believe that if they want to. What I am saying is to stop forcing me, via US tax dollars, to support and participate in your religious land grab at the expense of the livelyhood and human rights of others.

A couple of final clarifications that I hope are not necessary, but I will make them explicit. None of this means that I do not support the existence of Israel. None of this means that I support Hamas.
